DO's ✅

1. Think before you post — Ask yourself if the content is appropriate, accurate, and something you would be comfortable with your professor or future employer seeing.

2. Keep your profiles professional — Use your real name, a decent profile photo, and a bio that reflects your academic and career interests.

3. Verify information before sharing — Always check whether news, statistics, or claims are from a credible source before resharing them on any platform.

4. Use strong and unique passwords — Set a different password for each social media account and enable two-factor authentication wherever possible.

5. Report harmful or abusive content — Use the platform's reporting tools if you encounter cyberbullying, hate speech, fraud, or any form of online abuse.

6. Keep personal accounts private when needed — Adjust your privacy settings so that only people you trust can see your personal posts and information.

7. Give credit when sharing others' work — Always tag or mention the original creator when reposting photos, videos, or written content.

DON'Ts ❌

1. Don't share your location publicly — Avoid posting real-time location updates or tagging your exact address, hostel, or home on public posts.

2. Don't post in anger or frustration — Emotional posts made in haste can damage your reputation permanently. Take time to cool down before responding to conflicts online.

3. Don't spread unverified news or rumours — Sharing false information, even unintentionally, can have serious legal and social consequences.

4. Don't accept friend or follow requests from strangers — Unknown accounts may be fake profiles used for phishing, scamming, or social engineering attacks.

5. Don't share personal or financial details online — Never post your Aadhaar number, bank details, UPI ID, OTP, or phone number on any social media platform.

6. Don't engage with or provoke online trolls — Arguing with hostile users online rarely leads to anything productive. Block and report instead.

7. Don't use social media during academic hours irresponsibly — Excessive use during lectures or exams affects your concentration and academic performance.
