CASE STUDY- UPI PAYMENT FRAUD

What is UPI Payment Fraud?

UPI payment fraud is when someone tricks you into sending them money or giving them your payment details. They do this by lying to you or pretending to be someone.

Scenario:

There was a girl named Riya. She was a 19-year- college student from Bhopal. Riya put her laptop up for sale on OLX. Then a man named Vikram sent her a message. He said he was an Army officer and he really wanted to buy her laptop.

How It Happened. Step by Step:

-First Vikram sent Riya a message on WhatsApp. He said he would pay her the amount right away using UPI.

-Then he sent her an Army ID card. He was very polite and confident. This made Riya trust him.

-Next Vikram told Riya that he had already sent her ₹25,000. He asked her to accept the payment by scanning a QR code he sent her.

-The QR code was not really a payment. It was a link that would take money from her account. When Riya entered her UPI PIN she was actually sending money to Vikram not receiving it.

-After that ₹18,000 was taken out of Riyas account away. Then Vikrams phone was turned off.

Who is Usually Targeted?

* College students like Riya who sell things on OLX and Facebook Marketplace

* People who use UPI for the time and do not know how QR codes work

* People who need money badly and might do something without checking

Consequences

* You lose money. It is hard to get it back when it is sent through UPI.

* You feel bad and worried. You do not trust digital payments anymore.

* It is hard to report the crime and find the person who did it.

* If you are a student losing money can make it hard for you to pay for things you need.

Source

This is a story based on things that have happened to people, in India. You can find information at cybercrime.gov.in under "Online Job and Shopping Frauds".
