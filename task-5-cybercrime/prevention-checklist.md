General Cybersecurity Tips:

-You should use strong and unique passwords for every single account. These passwords need to be at 12 characters long and have a mix of uppercase letters, lowercase letters, numbers and symbols. Never use the password for multiple accounts.

-It is an idea to enable two-factor authentication on all your important accounts like Gmail, Instagram, LinkedIn and GitHub. This adds a layer of security even if someone gets your password.

-If you get a link or attachment from someone you do not know do not click on it. These links can look real. They are actually trying to steal your login information.

-Before you enter any information on a website make sure it is safe. Check that the website address starts with "https://" and look for the icon in the address bar.

-Keep your phone and laptop updated with the software and security updates. Old software can have problems that hackers can use to get into your accounts.

-Be careful what you share on media. Do not post your phone number, college ID, home address or daily routine for everyone to see. Bad people can use this information to target you.

-Only use Wi-Fi networks that you trust. Do not use public Wi-Fi for banking or logging into your accounts. Use your data or a VPN instead.

-Only download apps from the Google Play Store or Apple App Store. Do not install apps from sources because they can have hidden malware or spyware.

Financial and UPI Safety Tips are very important:

-Never share your UPI PIN, OTP or CVV with anyone. No bank or government agency will ever ask for this information over a call or message. If someone asks for it it is a scam so hang up immediately.

-You need to understand how UPI works. When you receive money you do not need to enter your UPI PIN or scan a QR code. If someone asks you to do this to "accept" a payment you are actually sending money not receiving it. This is a scam that targets students.

How to Report Cybercrime in India:

If you or someone you know has been a victim of cybercrime including UPI fraud, phishing or online harassment you can report it.

-You can file a complaint on the portal: https://cybercrime.gov.in. This portal is available 24 hours a day. Make sure you have screenshots transaction IDs and chat records ready before you file.

-You can also call the number 1930 anytime to report cyber fraud. This is especially helpful for UPI fraud.

-If you are on campus you can report to your colleges IT department or student grievance cell, for help and support.
